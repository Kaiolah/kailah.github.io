assignment 1

In small groups of 2 or 3, students will work together to establish a system that individual students will then utilize to recreate an image with Binary, ASCII, or Unicode characters. Groups may reconvene to reassess their group’s systems or individual students may “fork the code” (within reason) to better serve their images. Final text images must be a minimum of 60 character rows/columns along the shortest edge.

  • Become familiar with a sandbox development environment
  • Explore the foundation and utility of systems and languages
  • In a group, develop an adequate and unique system that can be used to clearly and/or creatively communicate your groups’ images (10 pts)
  • Optimize your system for your individual image (10 pts)
  • In critique, clearly discuss and communicate your developed system as related to your text drawing (5 pts)
  • Consider inline styling and spans (Extra: +1-5 points)

    • assignment 2

    The class will work together to build an assemblage sculpture from found and classroom materials. Individual students will then create a “CSS Still Life” that must consider HTML layout, nesting, and CSS styling. Final work may employ CSS animations and any degree of design reduction and abstraction. All HTML & CSS drawings must be horizontally oriented at 800x600px.

  • Work together to build an assemblage of symbolic materials (1 pt)
  • Discover and apply the basics of HTML and CSS languages as layout and styling tools (1 pt)
  • Utilize core design principles (such as framing, closed vs. open composition, reduction, and abstraction) to creatively interpret a collective artwork (3-dimensional) as personal representation (2-dimensional) (5 pts)
  • Employ modes creative problem solving, working within limitations (5 pts)
  • Clearly communicate your ideas, process, and execution in critique (5 pts)

    • Your CSS Still Life must be contained within a horizontal 800 x 600 px div and demonstrate at least 1 instance of these criteria (1 pt each):
  • Divs
  • Classes
  • Positioning (absolute, relative, fixed)
  • Padding
  • Margins
  • Background-color
  • Background: linear-gradient
  • Pseudo-classes (before, after, hover, etc)
  • Explore CSS animations (Extra: +1-3 pts)

    • final assignment

    Students will create an online publication (fan site, zine, exhibition, etc) that includes a minimum of 3 separate pages and a navigational tool/toolset. No single page and its linked content can be larger than 5 megabytes.

  • Choose a topic for your publication, and establish a consistent visual style to deliver uniform messaging and theming (10 pts)
  • Interpret the webpage format to creatively present your topic's information to a viewer (10 pts)
  • What is the flow of information (steady or dynamic)? What is your navigation system between pages? Demonstrate intentional strategies to guide a viewer through your pages by exploring the principles of sequence: cadence, rhythm, pause, punctuation and irregularity, logical order (or intentional disorder), etc. (10 pts)
  • At least one page of your publication must utilize a grid/column layout: tables, vw/percent with floats, flexbox, or CSS grid (10 pts)
  • Organize your files and directories by instituting and delivering an effective, logical schema (5 pts)
  • Direct the class through your intended site navigation during critique (5 pts)
