morrison: mothering beyond the biological

Morrison did not only raise her own two sons, but raised a generation of black authors. As an editor at Random House, she used her role as a platform to uplift writers around her. She published authors such as Gayl Jones, Toni Cade Bambara, Angela Davis, Chinua Achebe, and many others. Some of the authors she published had never been published in the United States before, which opened a new audience for them as writers. Outside of their work relationships, Morrison acted as a pillar of support, using her extra money to give to writers. Through this, she alongside a generation of brilliant Black authors, gave new life to Black women’s fiction and storytelling.
